What FOSR is

A 20-minute assessment.
A personal report per founder.
A cohort picture before day one.

What this means in practice

A founder completes a 25-question assessment. It takes 20 minutes. They answer based on what they actually do — not what they intend or know. Inala produces a report showing where their financial decision-making is strong, where it is developing, and the one move that will shift the most from where they are right now.

FOSR is built on the Decision Gate Framework — Inala's proprietary model of financial decision-making. It identifies which of four structural areas is most developed, which has the most room to grow, and what kind of intervention will actually land for this founder at this point in their business.

1
A scored profile across four areas
Showing where each founder's financial decision-making is strong and where it has the most room to grow — before your programme begins.
2
Financial Mindset Score
A separate score measuring how each founder relates to their financial information — not what they know, but whether they engage with it. This determines which intervention will land.
3
The specific area with the most room to grow
Not a generic financial health grade. One named development area per founder — with a plain-language explanation of what shifting it would produce for their business.
4
One first move and up to three flags
One specific, actionable first move for this founder right now. Up to three flags identifying structural conditions your programme or funder needs to know about before proceeding.
The problem FOSR solves

Every programme faces
the same three problems.
FOSR addresses all three.

The founders in your cohort each arrive with a different structural capacity. Without a diagnostic that maps that capacity, you are sequencing curriculum by assumption. FOSR gives you the structural picture before day one — so you build from where each founder actually is.

The wasted spend problem

"We invested in this cohort and half of them didn't implement anything. We can't explain why to our funders."

A meaningful portion of every cohort needs a different kind of support before they can absorb what you deliver. FOSR maps structural readiness per founder — before you sequence a single session.

The unexplained variance problem

"Same programme. Same facilitators. Completely different outcomes. We have no structural explanation."

Without a structural capacity baseline, variance is attributed to founder motivation. FOSR gives you the actual structural explanation — and the intelligence to sequence better next cycle.

The accountability gap

"Our funder wants impact metrics. We have completion rates. That's not the same thing."

FOSR produces a before-and-after structural measure. The score shift is a measurable, defensible impact metric completion rates cannot produce.

The instrument

Four pillars.
One Mindset Score.
One precise structural picture.

FOSR measures financial decision behaviour — not knowledge, attitude, or financial health. Each pillar asks a simple question about what actually happens in the business. The answers are behaviourally anchored: not what the founder knows, but what they do.

1
P1 · Financial Visibility · 30%
Financial Visibility — the capacity to know what is actually happening right now
Can they tell you — without looking anything up — whether the business will run out of cash in 60 days? This is the foundation all other capacity builds on.
Cash position · Revenue pattern · Pipeline · Runway · Forward obligations
30%
2
P2 · Signal Absorption · 30%
Signal Absorption — the capacity to read financial information as a story, not just a number
A founder with strong P2 capacity does not just check figures — they read them as signals about what is happening in the business and what to do next.
Variance investigation · Revenue signals · Profitability · Trend recognition
30%
3
P3 · Behavioural Integration · 20%
Behavioural Integration — the capacity to bring financial thinking into real decisions
When pricing a job, hiring someone, or pursuing an opportunity — a founder with strong P3 capacity uses financial data, not just instinct, to decide.
Cost decisions · Pricing from floor · Collections · Opportunity assessment · Slow period response
20%
4
P4 · Decision Alignment · 20%
Decision Alignment — the capacity to hold financial logic under pressure
When cash is tight or a client pushes on price — a founder with strong P4 capacity makes decisions from their financial picture, not from urgency or anxiety.
Pressure response · Price negotiation · Cost terms · Unexpected outflows · Compliance posture
20%
★
Financial Mindset Score — the relationship with financial data that determines what will land
The Mindset Score measures how each founder currently relates to their financial information — not what they know, but whether they engage with it honestly and regularly. A founder who avoids their bank statements will not use a cash flow tool, no matter how good the training is. The Mindset Score tells your team who is ready for tools and habit-building, and who needs something to shift first at a more fundamental level. It is reported separately from the structural score because it is a different kind of finding — and requires a different kind of response.

Our story

Built from three angles.
None of them theoretical.

Angle one — the employed accountant

Close enough to the numbers to know exactly what they reveal. Far enough from the decisions to watch them get ignored anyway.

Angle two — the founder in incubation

A real business, a real programme, and the discovery that knowing how to read finances and actually reading them are two very different things.

Angle three — the accountant running their own business

All the tools. All the training. And still, some months, the cobbler's children had no shoes. That is where the question behind FOSR came from — not what founders are missing, but what capacity they already have and why it is not yet fully deployed.

FOSR came out of all three. It is a diagnostic — not a training programme, not a checklist. It shows you exactly where each founder's financial decision-making is strong, where it is developing, and what one move would shift the most.
