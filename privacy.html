Privacy Policy
Effective date: 1 April 2026 · Last updated: 1 April 2026

1. Who we are

This Privacy Policy applies to Inala Advisory (Pty) Ltd, trading as Inala Financial Diagnostics (referred to in this policy as "Inala", "we", "us", or "our"). We are a South African registered company providing financial diagnostic services to enterprise development programmes and development finance institutions.

We are committed to protecting the personal information of all individuals who interact with our website, our assessment instrument, and our services, in accordance with the Protection of Personal Information Act 4 of 2013 (POPIA) and other applicable data protection legislation.

2. What personal information we collect and why

From website visitors and enquirers — when you submit an enquiry through our contact form, we collect your name, email address, organisation name, and the content of your message. We use this information solely to respond to your enquiry and to follow up on the engagement you have requested. We do not add you to any mailing list without your consent.

From FOSR assessment respondents — when a founder or entrepreneur completes the FOSR Financial-Operational Signal Review assessment, we collect their name, business name, sector, years trading, and responses to the assessment questions. This information is used to produce a Curriculum Signal Output report for the assessed individual. Assessment responses are processed in accordance with the data processing agreement between Inala and the programme or institution that deployed the assessment.

3. How we use your information

  • To respond to enquiries submitted through our website
  • To produce FOSR assessment reports for individual founders
  • To produce Cohort Benchmark Reports for programme operators (using anonymised and aggregated data only)
  • To build and maintain a benchmark dataset of financial decision-making capacity in South African SMEs — using only anonymised and aggregated data that cannot identify any individual
  • To improve the FOSR instrument based on aggregated response patterns

We do not use your personal information for direct marketing, advertising, or any purpose not listed above without your explicit consent.

4. Individual assessment results — confidentiality

Individual FOSR assessment results are confidential. We do not share individual scores, pattern statements, or any identifying information from an individual's assessment with any third party other than the programme or institution that deployed the assessment and the assessed individual themselves. Individual results are never published, never sold, and never included in any public-facing report in any identifiable form.

5. Benchmark data

Inala publishes anonymised and aggregated benchmark findings — for example, sector-level averages of structural financial decision-making capacity across assessed cohorts. These publications contain no individual scores, no business names, and no information that could identify any assessed founder. All published findings are reviewed to confirm that no individual can be identified before publication.

6. How long we keep your information

Enquiry data from website contact forms is retained for 24 months from the date of enquiry, or until the engagement to which the enquiry relates has concluded, whichever is later. Assessment response data is retained for 5 years from the date of assessment to enable longitudinal research and instrument development. After this period, individual identifying information is removed and only anonymised data is retained.

7. Your rights under POPIA

You have the right to request access to the personal information we hold about you, to request correction of inaccurate information, and to request deletion of your personal information in certain circumstances. To exercise any of these rights, contact us at siphiwe@inalaadvisory.com. We will respond within 30 days.

8. Cookies and tracking

This website does not currently use cookies, analytics tracking, or any third-party tracking tools. We do not collect any information about visitors beyond what is explicitly submitted through our contact form.

9. Third-party services

Our contact form is processed by Formspree (formspree.io). Formspree receives your form submission data in order to deliver it to us. Formspree's privacy policy governs their handling of this data. We do not use any other third-party services that receive personal data from this website.

10. Security

We take reasonable technical and organisational measures to protect the personal information we hold against unauthorised access, loss, or misuse. Assessment responses are stored in access-controlled systems. No method of electronic transmission or storage is 100% secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.
