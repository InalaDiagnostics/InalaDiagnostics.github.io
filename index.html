Inala Advisory · positioning principle

The Decision Gate Framework is the proprietary architecture underlying FOSR. It operates on the principle that decision-making capacity is structural — built or unbuilt — not motivational or attitudinal. The four pillars map the conditions under which financial information enters, is processed, and influences decisions in a founder-led business. Pressure exposes which capacities are load-bearing; the consequences surface financially and operationally.

FOSR uses a non-linear scoring model where answer choice reflects structural capacity, not right or wrong. A Mindset Score is computed separately from five starred questions and determines the intervention type: Mindset Reframe, Habit Design, or Tool Introduction.

The pattern statement — analyst-written, specific to this founder's business — is what distinguishes FOSR from any automated assessment. It is the thing that makes a founder read their report and think: "that is exactly my business."

Instrument scope · important
FOSR is designed for founder-led businesses where the founder holds strategic, financial, and operational decision authority. The instrument maps the structural conditions of that specific decision layer. It is not suited to businesses with a delegated executive team — the decision layer is distributed and the instrument's architecture would not produce a valid signal.

Pillar 1 · 30% weight
Financial Visibility
Cash position · runway · revenue pattern
Does the founder hold a continuous mental model of their financial position — or do they go looking when a decision forces them to? The capacity to name where the business stands, without retrieval, is the foundation of every subsequent pillar.
Pillar 2 · 30% weight
Signal Absorption
Reads figures as narrative · not just records
Does financial data tell the founder a story about what to do next — or does it confirm what they already sense? Reading figures as a forward signal rather than a historical record is the most common structural gap in developing founder-led businesses.
Pillar 3 · 20% weight
Behavioural Integration
Financial data enters pricing · hiring · collections
Does financial information actually change what the founder does — in pricing decisions, hiring timing, collections behaviour? The gap between knowing and doing is structural. FOSR maps where that gap sits and what produced it.
Pillar 4 · 20% weight
Decision Alignment
Financial logic holds under pressure
Does the founder's financial logic hold when the business is under stress — a large client requesting a discount, tight payroll, a dry pipeline? Pressure doesn't create new patterns. It reveals which structural capacities were embedded and which were not.
