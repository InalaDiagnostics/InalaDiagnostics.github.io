Our story

We know this problem
from three angles.

Angle one — the employed accountant

Close enough to the numbers to know exactly what they reveal. Far enough from the decisions to watch them get ignored anyway.

Angle two — the founder in incubation

A big idea and a business plan that looked better on paper than in the bank account — learning the hard way that knowing how to read finances and actually reading them are two very different things.

Angle three — the accountant running their own business

Which turns out to be the most humbling combination of all. All the tools. All the training. And still, some months, the cobbler's children had no shoes.

FOSR came out of all three. Not a training programme. Not a checklist. A diagnostic built to name the specific structural condition between a founder and their financial decisions — and the first move to close it.

Financial Mindset Score — the mirror behind the mirror
The Financial Mindset Score measures each founder's current relationship with financial data — not what they know, but how they approach it. A founder who dreads opening their accounts will not use a cash flow tool no matter how good the training is. The Mindset Score shows your programme team which founders are ready to engage with structural tools — and which ones need their relationship with financial data to shift first. That distinction is what determines whether your intervention holds beyond the programme window.
