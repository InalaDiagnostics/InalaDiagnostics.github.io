The clean rule
A programme that already has FOSR Approach 1 from a previous cohort can add the Mentor App at R18,000 — their existing reports activate it. They don't repurchase the assessment. On renewal they move to a bundle. A new programme without any FOSR history must start with either the Pilot, the Starter bundle, or the Professional bundle. There is no Mentor App without an assessment, ever.

MEC · Mentorship Efficiency Credit
All pricing conversations for bundles should include the MEC framing. FOSR saves 3–5 hours of basic financial discovery per cohort member = R45,000–R75,000 of mentor time for a 20-member cohort. The Starter bundle at R32,000 pays for itself before the first session. The Professional bundle at R78,000 costs less than the mentor time it replaces. This is not a cost — it is a reallocation of existing spend toward higher-quality use of mentor hours.
International buyers · pricing and payment terms
Pricing outside South Africa is quoted in USD at a 10% uplift over the direct ZAR conversion. The uplift absorbs FX conversion spread (2–4%), receiving-bank inward wire fees (1–2%), and correspondent-bank slippage (2–4%). This is not a premium — it is the cost of making cross-border transactions net to the same effective receipt as a domestic ZAR sale. Standard convention in professional services sold across African markets.

Withholding tax · buyer responsibility
Several African jurisdictions apply withholding tax (typically 5–15%) on services rendered to local entities by foreign providers. Ghana, Kenya, Nigeria, Rwanda among others. Standard contract clause: buyer grosses up — withholding tax is paid on top of the agreed fee, not deducted from it. Double-taxation treaty benefits may apply where a DTA exists between South Africa and the buyer's jurisdiction; buyer is responsible for processing treaty-relief documentation. Confirm specific jurisdiction before signature.
Currency coverage across African markets
Default international currency: USD — appropriate for Anglophone Africa (Ghana, Kenya, Nigeria, Uganda, Rwanda, Ethiopia, Tanzania) and most institutional buyers funded by multilaterals (World Bank, USAID, GIZ, Global Affairs Canada). EUR available on request for Francophone West and Central Africa (Senegal, Côte d'Ivoire, Cameroon, Gabon — CFA franc is EUR-pegged). ZAR retained for SADC buyers with ZAR-denominated budgets, including SA regional offices of multinationals and certain SADC institutional buyers.
