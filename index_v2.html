The problem

Every programme has the same three problems.
None of them are about the founders.

The founders in your cohort are not all at the same structural stage. You are delivering as if they are. That is not a failing of your programme — it is the absence of a pre-programme diagnostic that tells you where each founder actually is before you sequence what they receive.

💸
The wasted spend problem

"We invested in this cohort and half of them didn't implement anything. We can't explain why to our funders."

A meaningful portion of every cohort is not structurally ready to absorb what you deliver. You spend the same per founder regardless. The spend is not the problem — the sequencing is.

📊
The unexplained variance problem

"Same programme. Same facilitators. Completely different outcomes. We have no structural explanation for the gap."

Without a pre-programme diagnostic, you have no structural explanation for the variance. You attribute it to founder commitment or capability. FOSR gives you the actual explanation.

📋
The accountability gap

"Our funder wants impact metrics. We have completion rates. That's not the same thing and we both know it."

Completion rates and survival statistics tell funders the programme ran — not that it changed anything. FOSR produces a before-and-after structural measure that does.

FOSR measures how financial information moves through a founder's decisions — across four structural pillars and a Financial Mindset Score. It produces a Curriculum Signal Output for each founder: their structural profile, the specific barrier holding their business back right now, and the intervention that will land given where they actually are. Not where you assume they are.

Funder-reportable impact metric
Re-administer at programme exit. The shift in structural score is a measurable, defensible impact metric that completion rates cannot produce.

The instrument

Four structural pillars.
One Financial Mindset Score.
Five score bands.

FOSR measures financial decision behaviour across four structural domains, weighted by their foundational importance. The Financial Mindset Score runs alongside — not folded into the structural score — because mindset is a precondition for structural behaviour, not a component of it.

P1
Financial Visibility
Can your founder tell you — without looking anything up — whether their business will run out of cash in the next 60 days?
Cash position · Runway · Debtor status · Forward visibility
30%
P2
Signal Absorption
When the monthly figures arrive, does your founder read them to understand the business — or check the bottom line and close the email?
Variance investigation · Payment terms · Revenue signals · Profitability clarity
30%
P3
Behavioural Integration
When your founder hires someone or prices a job — does financial data enter that decision, or does instinct?
Cost decisions · Collections · Budget use · Opportunity assessment
20%
P4
Decision Alignment
Under pressure — cash tight, client pushing back, fast decision required — does financial logic hold or does urgency override it?
Pressure response · Terms assessment · Cost floor · Compliance posture
20%
🧠
Financial Mindset Score — reported alongside the structural profile
The Financial Mindset Score measures each founder's current relationship with financial data — not what they know, but how they approach it. A founder who dreads opening their accounts will not use a cash flow tool no matter how good the training is. A founder who is curious about their numbers will. The Mindset Score tells your programme team which founders are ready to engage with structural tools — and which ones need their relationship with financial data to shift first.
